The Visual Machine Learning Explainer

Drag the data. Move the sliders. Watch the models change. Understand why each method makes the decisions it makes, before you touch a line of Python.

Most ML tutorials are walls of math or walls of code. Neither builds intuition. This tool flips that around. You drag data points, tune the actual scikit-learn hyperparameters, and watch eight foundational ML methods respond in real time.

Every slider is labeled with both the real parameter name (max_depth, alpha, n_neighbors) and a plain-language description of what it does. Every chart updates live. A narrative panel names what's happening as you work, flagging overfitting alerts, underfitting, good generalization, or L1 feature selection.

Built for anyone who wants to feel how machine learning models actually behave. No Python required, no math prerequisites, no setup. Runs entirely in your browser.

What to try first

  • Open Decision Trees, crank max_depth to 10, and watch the train-test gap turn red as the model overfits.
  • Open k-NN, turn scaling Off and push income_scale to 10×. The boundary collapses. This is a real production bug.
  • Open Evaluation, set FN cost $50 and FP cost $5, then click "Jump to cost-optimal threshold."
